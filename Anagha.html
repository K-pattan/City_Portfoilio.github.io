Smart City Services – Maharajpur

Recreation & Lifestyle

Overview

Recreation and lifestyle services include parks, walking tracks, open gyms, cultural spaces, and community halls that enhance physical, mental, and social well-being in a smart city.

Why this is needed

  • Urban lifestyle increases stress and health issues
  • Lack of green spaces reduces quality of life
  • Uneven distribution creates accessibility problems

Data Structures Used

  • Graph – zones and parks as nodes
  • Queue – level-wise traversal
  • Array – distance storage
Algorithm Used: Breadth First Search (BFS)
Reason: Ensures fair and equal accessibility from all zones
Public Safety & Intelligent Surveillance

Overview

Public safety integrates AI-based surveillance cameras, drone monitoring, emergency response systems, and centralized control centers.

How it helps the city

  • Real-time incident detection
  • Faster emergency response
  • Improved crime prevention

Data Structures Used

  • Graph – road network
  • Priority Queue – emergency routing
  • Array – distance tracking
Algorithm Used: Dijkstra’s Algorithm
Reason: Computes shortest and safest routes for emergency vehicles
Digital Services & Smart Mobility

Overview

Digital services include citizen portals, traffic apps, smart parking, metro schedules, and online grievance systems.

Problems Solved

  • Reduces paperwork and physical visits
  • Improves transparency
  • Provides real-time updates

Data Structures Used

  • Hash Tables – citizen records
  • Arrays – service requests
Algorithm Used: Hashing
Reason: Enables instant and secure access to digital records
Senior Citizen & Disabled-Friendly Services

Overview

These services prioritize elderly and differently-abled citizens in healthcare, transport, emergency, and government services.

Key Benefits

  • Reduced waiting time
  • Faster emergency handling
  • Inclusive city development

Data Structures Used

  • Priority Queue
  • Heap
Algorithm Used: Priority Queue (Heap)
Reason: Urgent and critical requests are handled first
Smart Water Management

Overview

Smart water management uses sensors, smart meters, and automated systems to monitor water usage and ensure efficient distribution.

City-Level Impact

  • Reduced water wastage
  • Early leak detection
  • Fair water distribution

Data Structures Used

  • Array – consumption data
  • Graph – water distribution network
Algorithm Used: Greedy Algorithm
Reason: Allocates water optimally based on demand and priority
