Smart City Services – Maharajpur

Recreation & Lifestyle

Parks, walking tracks, and community spaces improve physical and mental well-being. Facilities are distributed evenly across city zones to ensure accessibility.

Purpose: Promote health and social interaction. Implementation: Place parks optimally using city mapping.

Algorithm: Breadth First Search (BFS)
Usage: Ensures equal reachability from residential zones.
Time Complexity: O(V + E)

Public Safety & Services

Includes AI cameras, drone monitoring, emergency units, and service centers. The system ensures rapid response to incidents anywhere in the city.

Purpose: Minimize response time, monitor traffic and events.
Implementation: AI cameras for real-time detection, drones for surveillance, shortest path routing for emergency vehicles.

Algorithm: Dijkstra’s Algorithm
Usage: Computes shortest paths for emergency and drone routing.
Time Complexity: O(E log V)

Digital Services

Centralized platforms: citizen portal, traffic app, smart parking, metro scheduling. Allows residents to report issues, check schedules, and book services.

Purpose: Efficient and transparent service delivery.
Implementation: Digital dashboard integrating all city services.

Algorithm: Hashing
Usage: Fast retrieval of citizen records, traffic updates, and metro schedules.
Time Complexity: O(1)

Senior Citizen & Disabled-Friendly Services

Priority service systems ensure elderly and differently-abled individuals receive timely assistance, e.g., accessible transport, medical services, dedicated queues.

Purpose: Inclusivity and prompt attention.
Implementation: Requests handled based on urgency and priority.

Algorithm: Priority Queue (Heap)
Usage: Handles service requests by urgency.
Time Complexity: O(log n)

Smart Water Management

Monitors supply, detects leaks, and distributes water efficiently. Sensors collect data to allocate resources optimally.

Purpose: Sustainable water usage, reduce wastage, ensure supply.
Implementation: Sensors, smart valves, and predictive analysis for allocation.

Algorithm: Greedy Technique
Usage: Allocates water based on demand and priority.
Time Complexity: O(n)
